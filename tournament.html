ETDS

The ETDS also known as the European Tournament for Dancing Students is a large ballroom dancing tournament attracting hundreds of dancers from Europe. The tournament has been hosted twice a year since 1989. This time the ETDS will compete for 'Der Maus' in Amsterdam.

All dancers can compete in the tournament, which is spread over two days. For each dancer there are two competitions to join, Standard and Latin. The Standard dances consisting out of Slow Waltz, Tango, Quickstep, and for higher levels Slow Foxtrot and the Viennese Waltz. The Latin dances consist out of Chachacha, Rumba, Jive, and for higher levels, Samba and Paso doble.

This tournament is not only about competing. The are a multitude of fun activities planned, and two great parties.

Classes

There are two starting groups: Breitensport and CloseD/Open. If you're are a recreational dancer (non-competition) or beginner you're probably in the Breitensport. If you dance competitions, it will be depending on your competition level. Check the overview at the bottom of the page.

Breitensport

This class contains subclasses (in ascending level): Amateurs, Professionals, Masters and Champions.
There will be a qualification round with all Breitensport dancers, to determine in which level you will continue the competition.

Blind dating

No partner? You can still sign up!
The lottery system will equally distribute leaders and followers and people do not mess with the system (by not showing etc, SO DON'T!), there will be a dance partner for everyone! To find a partner we have blind date rounds on Friday and Saturday evening. During these rounds all without a partner (or mandatory blinddaters) are recognizably marked for you to invite to dance with you. If you like the specimen you are dancing with, you can together register at the tournament office.

Mandatory Blind Dating

For the (super) experienced of us dancers there is a mandatory blind dating. This way you will have another face to look at, then that of your usual dance partner. But above all, then you wont be so darn good. Give other people also a chance to win! Or you can try to defeat your partner.

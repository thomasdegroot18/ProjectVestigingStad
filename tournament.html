ETDS

The ETDS also known as the European Tournament for Dancing Students is a large ballroom dancing tournament attracting hunderds of dancers from Europe. The tournament is hosted twice a year since 1989. This time the ETDS will compete for 'Der Maus' in Amsterdam.

All dancers can compete in the tournament, which is spread over two days. For each dancer there are two competitions to join, standard and latin. The standard dances consisting out of English Waltz, Tango, Quickstep, and for higher levels Slow Foxtrot and and Viennise Waltz. The Latin dances consist out of Chachacha, Rumba, Jive, and for higher levels, Samba and Paso doble.

This tournament is not only about competing. The are an multitude of fun activities planned, and two great parties.

Classes

There are two main classes: Breitensport and CloseD/Open. If your are a recreational dancer (non-competion) or beginner you're probably in the Breitensport. If you dance competitions, it will be depending on your competition level. Check the overview at the bottom of the page.

Breitensport

This class contains subclasses (in ascending level): Amateurs, Professionals, Masters and Champions.
There will be a qualification round with all Breitersport dancers, to determine in which level you will continue the competition.

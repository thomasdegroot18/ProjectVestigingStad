INDEX/HOME: The ETDS also known as the European Tournament for Dancing Students is a large ballroom dancing tournament attracting hunderds of dancers from Europe. The tournament is hosted twice a year since 1989. This time the ETDS will compete for 'Der Maus' in Amsterdam. All dancers can compete in the tournament, which is spread over two days. For each dancer there are two competitions to join, standard and latin. The standard dances consisting out of English Waltz, Tango, Quickstep, and for higher levels Slow Foxtrot and and Viennise Waltz. The Latin dances consist out of Chachacha, Rumba, Jive, and for higher levels, Samba and Paso doble. This tournament is not only about competing. The are an multitude of fun activities planned, and two great parties. We are happy to host the ETDS 2020 for you! The ETDS will start from May 29th to June 1st, 2020. Hopefully we will see you all there! LOCATION:

How to get there

By car

By car A1

Use the right lane to take the Ring-Oost/A10/E35 exit toward Rotterdam/Luchthaven Schiphol, then keep right, follow signs for Watergraafsmeer.

By car A2

At the interchange Knooppunt Amstel, Use the right lanes to follow signs for E35/A10 toward Zaanstad. Then take exit s113 to Watergraafsmeer.

By train - from Central Station Amsterdam

From Amsterdam central it is only two trainstops to Amsterdam Science park.

By Bus - from Amstel Station

Parking

We have limited parking available at the location Science Park. It is possible to park around the Science Park, but it will be payed parking. To circumvent paying expensive tickets it is recommended to park at station Weesp. An direct train from Weesp will bring you to Amsterdam Science Park in under 10 minutes.
